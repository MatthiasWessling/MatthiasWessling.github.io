These sources document the life and intellectual contributions of Naomi Klein, a prominent Canadian journalist, author, and activist recognized for her critiques of neoliberalism and corporate globalization. Her seminal work, No Logo, established her as a leading voice against brand-driven consumerism and labor exploitation in the late 1990s. Subsequent major publications, such as The Shock Doctrine and This Changes Everything, explore how "disaster capitalism" exploits crises and how the global economic system drives climate change. More recent projects, including the book Doppelganger and her multimedia series Unshocked, analyze modern political polarization, digital misinformation, and the rise of far-right ideologies.
